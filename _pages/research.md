## Overview

My research develops machine learning methods for understanding complex biological systems. I work at the intersection of artificial intelligence, graph learning, and computational biology, with applications in spatial omics and single-cell genomics.

The long-term goal of my research is to develop computational tools that enable scientific discovery from large-scale biological datasets.

## Research Area 1: Spatial Omics

Spatial omics technologies measure molecular activity while preserving spatial organization within tissues.

My work develops computational approaches for:

* Spatial transcriptomics analysis
* Cellular interaction modeling
* Tissue organization studies
* Spatial representation learning

## Research Area 2: Single-Cell Genomics

Single-cell technologies provide unprecedented insight into cellular heterogeneity.

Research directions include:

* Cell type identification
* Cell-state transition analysis
* Data integration across studies
* Multi-omic data fusion

## Research Area 3: Graph Machine Learning

Many biological systems can naturally be represented as graphs.

Current interests include:

* Graph neural networks
* Biological network analysis
* Knowledge graphs
* Explainable graph learning

## Research Area 4: Trustworthy AI for Scientific Discovery

Scientific applications require models that are robust, interpretable, and reproducible.

Research topics include:

* Model interpretability
* Uncertainty quantification
* Reproducible machine learning
* Responsible AI for biomedical applications
